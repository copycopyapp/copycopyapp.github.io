Privacy

What happens to your voice.

Last updated: June 2026

TimerTalk lets you set reminders by speaking them, and plays your recording back when the alarm fires. This explains exactly what the app does with your microphone, your voice, and your reminders.

The short version: everything happens on your device. TimerTalk has no servers, no accounts, and no analytics. The developer cannot hear your recordings, read your reminders, or access any of your data. The one thing TimerTalk keeps is your voice recordings. They live on your device as the sound your alarms play, and they never leave it.

Microphone, recording, and speech recognition

TimerTalk uses your microphone when you record a reminder.

  • Your recording is saved on your device and used as the sound the alarm plays back. It is stored locally within the app and is never uploaded.
  • TimerTalk transcribes your recording to pull out the reminder and the timing. It requests Apple's on-device speech recognition and uses it whenever your device supports it for your language, so the audio stays on your device.
  • On devices or languages where on-device recognition isn't available, transcription is handled by Apple's speech-recognition service, under Apple's privacy policy. (TimerTalk currently supports English.)
  • The microphone is active only while you are recording a reminder. TimerTalk does not listen in the background.

Understanding what you say (on-device AI)

To turn something like “every weekday at 7, take your vitamins” into the right alarm, TimerTalk interprets the transcribed text.

  • When available, this runs on Apple's on-device Foundation Models (Apple Intelligence). It all happens on your device, with nothing sent to the developer or any third party.
  • On devices without it, TimerTalk uses a built-in text parser that also runs entirely on your device.
  • Either way, no part of your reminder is sent off your device for interpretation.

Your reminders and recordings

  • Your alarms, reminder text, notes, recordings, and settings are stored on your device.
  • Deleting an alarm deletes its recording. Uninstalling TimerTalk removes its recordings and settings.
  • None of this is backed up to the developer or synced to any server.

Alarms and notifications

TimerTalk schedules alarms through Apple's AlarmKit, with local notifications as a fallback. These are handled by iOS on your device; no alarm or notification content is sent anywhere.

No data collection

TimerTalk does not collect, access, or receive any of your data. There are no servers, no accounts, and no cloud sync. The developer has no visibility into your reminders, your recordings, or how you use the app.

No analytics or tracking

TimerTalk contains no analytics, tracking, advertising, or crash-reporting code, and no third-party SDKs.

Permissions, and changing your mind

TimerTalk asks for microphone, speech recognition, and notification access so it can record your reminders, understand them, and alert you at the right time. You can grant or revoke any of these at any time in the iOS Settings app, under TimerTalk. If you revoke a permission, the related feature stops working until you grant it again.

Children's privacy

TimerTalk does not collect any personal information from anyone, including children.
